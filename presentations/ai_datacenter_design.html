SCALE

The Numbers Are Insane

Per-Rack Power

  • 2015 cloud rack: 10 kW
  • 2024 NVL72 rack: 120 kW
  • 2026 Rubin rack: ~200 kW
  • Custom designs: 300–500 kW

Per-Site Power

  • Old datacenter: 10–50 MW
  • Modern AI site: 1 GW
  • Stargate target: 5 GW+
  • Comparable to small nuclear plants

Investment

  • $500B+ committed by hyperscalers
  • Multi-year build cycles
  • Largest physical infra category in tech

POWER

Power Delivery

The Infrastructure

  • Connect to 115 kV+ grid
  • Multiple transformer stages
  • Busways instead of cables (current limits)
  • Multi-MW UPS + diesel generators
  • Battery storage growing

The Reliability Need

  • Training jobs run for weeks/months
  • Single power loss = millions in lost work
  • UPS sized for several minutes
  • Generators for indefinite operation

GRID

The Interconnection Bottleneck

The New Constraint

  • Building a 1 GW datacenter needs 1 GW of new utility capacity
  • New transmission, substations, generation
  • Process takes years
  • Now the gating factor on construction

Where Sites Cluster

  • Texas (cheap power, available)
  • Virginia (existing fiber)
  • Pacific NW (hydroelectric)
  • Arizona (cheap land)
  • Northern Europe (cool, renewable)
  • Middle East (solar + capital)

Immersion200+ kW/rackHighest density, messier maintenance

Why Air Cooling Fails

To remove 120 kW from one rack you’d need gale-force airflow at very low temperatures. Fan power consumption defeats itself. Air is limited to ~25–30 kW/rack with aggressive engineering. Below modern AI rack densities.

WATER

Water Consumption

The Cost

  • 1 GW datacenter consumes millions of gallons/day
  • Comparable to a small city
  • Mostly evaporative cooling tower makeup
  • Becoming controversial in dry regions

Mitigations

  • Dry cooling (more electricity, less water)
  • Wastewater reuse
  • Direct air rejection
  • Heat reuse in district heating (Europe)

NETWORK

Networking the Cluster

Intra-Datacenter

  • InfiniBand or RoCE
  • 400 Gbps/GPU current
  • 800 Gbps/GPU next gen
  • Petabit-per-second aggregate
  • 5–10% of total facility power

Inter-Datacenter

  • Dedicated long-haul fiber
  • Multi-Tbps links between sites
  • Latency limits parallelism strategies
  • Most training still single-site

BUILDING

The Physical Plant

Structure

  • Floor loads 4–5 tons per rack
  • Thicker concrete, more steel
  • Retrofitting old datacenters often impractical
  • 1 GW site = campus, not building

Modular Designs

  • Prefabricated modules
  • Faster build, incremental expansion
  • OpenAI Stargate concept

Site Selection Factors

  • Power availability
  • Water availability
  • Network connectivity
  • Climate
  • Land cost
  • Tax incentives
  • Latency to users (inference)

SUMMARY

The New Industrial Reality

What’s True

  • AI datacenters are a discrete shift in computing infrastructure
  • Power, cooling, networking, grid interactions all newly constrained
  • Investment is enormous; timelines are compressed
  • Geopolitically and environmentally consequential

Practitioner’s Lesson

For most users, the relevance of datacenter design is indirect — capacity and cost flow through the cloud. For those building infrastructure, the details determine viability. Decisions being made about AI datacenter construction over the next several years will shape the practical limits of what AI can do — and what it costs the rest of the world to make it happen.

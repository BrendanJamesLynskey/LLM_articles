CONTEXT

From Follower to Frontier

Through 2023, Chinese LLMs were assumed to lag the West. By late 2024, that assumption collapsed. Chinese open releases now match or exceed the best Western open models.

The Turning Points

  • DeepSeek-V3 (Dec 2024) — matched GPT-4 class at $5.5M training cost
  • DeepSeek-R1 (Jan 2025) — matched o1 reasoning, fully open
  • Qwen series — most-downloaded family on HF
  • Multiple labs releasing prolifically

What Changed

  • Technical capability — real, not gimmicky
  • Engineering quality high
  • Documentation thorough
  • Permissive licenses
  • Strategic implications for the industry

DEEPSEEK

DeepSeek

The Organization

  • Subsidiary of High-Flyer (quant trading firm)
  • Small team, dramatic outsized impact
  • Emphasis on transparency and reproducibility

DeepSeek-V3

  • 671B params, 37B active per token (MoE)
  • 14.8T tokens trained
  • ~$5.5M reported training cost
  • Trained on H800s under export controls

DeepSeek-R1

  • Matched o1 reasoning
  • Open weights, permissive license
  • Recipe: GRPO + binary rewards on math/code
  • No labeled CoT data needed
  • Most-cited LLM paper of 2025

The Lesson

Frontier reasoning is not exclusive to well-funded American labs.

QWEN

Qwen (Alibaba)

The Most Prolific

  • Largest, most maintained Chinese open LLM family
  • Sizes 0.5B → 72B+
  • Specialized variants: Qwen-Coder, Qwen-VL, Qwen-Reasoning
  • New releases every few months

Position

  • Most downloaded Chinese LLM on Hugging Face
  • Comparable to Llama community in the West
  • Many fine-tunes and merges based on Qwen
  • Strong in Chinese, English, multilingual

INNOVATIONS

Technical Contributions

From DeepSeek

  • MLA — Multi-head Latent Attention (4–10× KV cache compression)
  • GRPO — the dominant reasoning RL method
  • FP8 training at 600B+ scale
  • MoE optimizations

From the Broader Ecosystem

  • Long-context techniques (Kimi 200K+, Qwen million-token)
  • MoE routing innovations
  • Bilingual / multilingual training methodology
  • Cost-efficient training recipes

Note on MLA

Adopted by other model developers globally with credit. Particularly valuable for long-context inference where KV cache size dominates.

STRATEGY

Why Open-Source?

Ecosystem Building

  • Become the default in China and Asia
  • Train next-gen of Chinese AI engineers on their models
  • Build downstream applications
  • Same playbook as Meta with Llama

Compute Constraints

  • US export controls on advanced chips
  • Forced efficiency — can’t waste FLOPs
  • Paradox: controls accelerated efficiency innovation

Government Priorities

  • National AI strategy since 2017
  • Soft power and influence
  • Reduce dependence on Western providers
  • Less politically sensitive than commercial sales

GEOPOLITICS

The Complications

Trust & Provenance

  • Frontier models too large to fully audit
  • No backdoors found in major releases
  • Inherent uncertainty for risk-averse deployments

Censorship

  • Refuse sensitive Chinese political topics
  • Explicit training choice, not hidden
  • Targets political content, not capability
  • Irrelevant for most use cases

Regulatory

  • US rules in flux on adversarial-country AI
  • Compliance picture evolving
  • Most acute for high-stakes deployments

CALCULUS

The Practical Decision

Where Chinese Models Win

  • Cost-sensitive deployments (free weights)
  • Multilingual applications
  • Coding and math reasoning
  • Long context (Kimi-class)
  • Chinese-language NLP

Where Western May Win

  • English-language nuance
  • Latest features and APIs
  • Regulated industries with compliance constraints
  • Need to discuss politically sensitive topics

SUMMARY

The New Reality

What’s True

  • Chinese open LLMs are real, capable, and competitive
  • Technical contributions (MLA, GRPO, FP8 training) shape the global field
  • Locus of innovation is broadening — no longer one-region story
  • Open ecosystem is healthier and more diverse than ever

Practitioner’s Lesson

Evaluate Chinese models on their technical merits and choose them when they fit. They’re not subservient followers and in many cases are the best option for specific use cases. Geopolitical concerns are real but bounded to specific contexts. For most builders, the right approach is technical, not political.

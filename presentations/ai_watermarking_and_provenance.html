CONTEXT

The Authenticity Crisis

By 2025, more than half of new text published online was AI-generated or modified. Image and video generation has scaled similarly. Detection-after-the-fact has lost the arms race.

Use Cases Demanding Solutions

  • Misinformation detection
  • Academic integrity
  • Training data hygiene (model collapse)
  • Legal & contractual compliance
  • Copyright and attribution

The Approach

  • Watermarking: embed signal during generation
  • Provenance: cryptographic metadata travels with content
  • Complementary, not competing

TEXT

Text Watermarking: Kirchenbauer Scheme

The Mechanism

  1. Pseudorandom function (seeded by previous tokens) splits vocab into “green” and “red” lists
  2. Bias logits to prefer green tokens at sampling
  3. Watermarked text has slight excess of green tokens
  4. Detector knows the function, computes the fraction

Properties

  • Detection is statistical — longer text = higher confidence
  • Hundreds of tokens needed for reliable detection
  • Cheap (just a logit modification)
  • No model changes required

SYNTHID

Google SynthID-Text

The Technique

  • Tournament-style sampling at each step
  • Watermark function selects between candidates via pseudorandom score
  • More robust than simple logit biasing
  • Invisible to readers

Deployment

  • Enabled by default in Gemini API
  • One of few production-scale watermarks
  • Detection key held by Google
  • API verification available

LIMITS

Why Text Watermarking Is Hard

Fundamental Limits

  • Paraphrasing degrades watermarks
  • Translation destroys them
  • Short outputs insufficient signal
  • Self-hosted models can disable

The Multi-LLM Problem

  • Watermarking only catches content from cooperative models
  • Switch to non-watermarking model = bypass
  • Most effective at closed-API level
  • Least effective in open-source ecosystem

IMAGES

Image & Video Watermarking

SynthID-Image

  • Pixel-level perturbations
  • Imperceptible to humans
  • Detected by trained classifier
  • Survives crop, resize, JPEG, color edits
  • Deployed in Imagen / Gemini

Diffusion-Native Watermarks

  • Bias initial noise sampling
  • Tree-Ring watermark: circular pattern in frequency domain
  • Pattern propagates through diffusion
  • Harder to remove without retraining

Audio & Video

Audio watermarking is mature (decades of work). Video adds temporal dimension on top of frame-level techniques.

C2PA

Content Provenance: C2PA

What C2PA Is

  • Cryptographically signed manifests attached to media
  • Coalition: Adobe, Microsoft, Intel, BBC, Sony, Truepic
  • Each tool adds its own signed manifest
  • Builds a chain of provenance

Manifest Contents

  • Source (camera, AI model, editing tool)
  • History of modifications
  • Authenticity claims
  • Cryptographic signatures — tampering detectable

The Difference

Watermarking = signal in the content itself. Provenance = metadata attached to the file. Complementary.

The Fundamental Limit

C2PA is opt-in. Strip the manifest or use a non-supporting tool and it’s gone. Provides strong guarantees when present, no information when absent.

REALITY

The Adversarial Picture

Determined Attackers Win

  • Watermark removal literature growing
  • Removing C2PA manifests is trivial
  • Not robust against sophisticated adversaries
  • Like DRM — doesn’t stop piracy entirely

Still Useful

  • Cooperative use cases work
  • Detection of accidental misattribution
  • Academic integrity in good-faith environments
  • Trust within controlled ecosystems

SUMMARY

Necessary But Not Sufficient

The Practical Recipe

  • For content you create: use the strongest available watermarking and provenance signals
  • For content you consume: treat as useful signals, not definitive proof
  • For policies: avoid depending on perfect detection
  • For AI development: default to watermarking; preserve C2PA metadata

The Bigger Picture

Content authenticity in the age of AI requires technical signals + social norms + institutional structures together. Watermarking and provenance are necessary parts of the larger picture but cannot solve the problem alone. Building the rest of the picture is one of the most important challenges of AI deployment over the next several years.
